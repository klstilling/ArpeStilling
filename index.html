Visualizing San Francisco Crime Data using the D3 JavaScript library

2A: One scatter plot
and two datasets

In the following plot each point corresponds to a San Fransisco police department district. A large point means a high total crime count in the district for the selected year. A small point means a low total crime count in the district for the selected year. The x-coordinate of a point is the number of recorded prostitution crimes for the selected year, and the y-coordinate is the number of recorded vehicle theft crimes for the selected year.

You can select another year by pressing the button, and you can hover over a point in the coordinate system to get a summary of total crime, prostitution incidents and vehicle theft incidents.

The scale of the axis have not been scaled. This results in a plot where it is much easier to see the change in the prostitution and vehicle theft categories. It is much easier to compare the change between the two years when the axis are kept on transition.

When we compare the years 2003 and 2015 we can see that for most districts the total crime is roughly the same, but the number of prostitution and vehicle theft incidents have been reduced. Most notably the 'Northern' and 'Mission' districts have greatly reduced their number of the forementioned incidents. When we look at the 'Southern' and 'Central' districts, the total number of crime incidents have actually increased while there have been a decrease in the two categories.

2B: Visualizing geodata

The following d3 visualization is created from geodata of San Francisco and the SFPD dataset. We cluster Prostitution crime incidents from the SFPD dataset based on a K-Means clustering algorithm. Click on the buttons in order to change the value of K for the K-means algorithm.

You can also hover over one of the buttons to get a quick overview of the K-means clustering with the K-value listed on the button.

The visualization illustrates that there seems to be atleast 3 clusters. One could argue that 4 clusters is more optimal, but when k=5 is chosen, we divide the top right cluster into two different clusters. This does not visually seem like a very good idea.
